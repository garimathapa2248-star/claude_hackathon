Capitalization Calculator

What is capitalization?
When you don't pay interest during school, it accumulates. At repayment start, all unpaid interest gets added to your principal — and you pay interest on that larger number for the full term.

Visa & Tuition Classification

For NJIT students on visa transitions — a $16,000/year question worth fighting for.

In-state vs. international tuition — $16,000/year at NJIT
If your visa status changes (e.g. H-1B dependent to F-1), you risk reclassification as international — unless you proactively submit NJ domicile documentation. Over 4 years that's $64,000. Many students lose in-state status simply because nobody told them to file.
F-1 students: you may still qualify for in-state rates
F-1 status alone doesn't automatically disqualify you from in-state tuition in NJ. If you or your parents have established NJ domicile — not just residence — you can file a classification appeal.
Document checklist — in-state tuition appeal
1
Proof of NJ domicile
NJ driver's license or state ID, lease or mortgage in your name (or parent's if dependent), showing this is your primary home — not just a mailing address.
2
12-month continuous NJ residency evidence
Utility bills, bank statements, or employer letters showing a consistent NJ address for 12+ consecutive months before the semester of your appeal.
3
Intent to remain in NJ documentation
NJ voter registration card, NJ state tax returns (Form NJ-1040), or a signed domicile declaration form from NJIT's Registrar's Office.
4
Complete visa status documentation
Current I-20, I-94 arrival/departure record, all USCIS approval notices for current and previous visa statuses, and passport biographic page.
5
Submit to NJIT Registrar — before semester billing deadline
Email registrar@njit.edu · Subject: "In-State Tuition Classification Appeal — [Name] — [NJIT ID]". Attach all docs. Follow up in 5 business days. Late submissions are almost never accepted.
Health insurance waiver — 2-week window only
NJIT auto-enrolls international students in university health insurance (~$2,500/yr). If you have comparable coverage, you can waive it — but only in the first 2 weeks of each semester.
